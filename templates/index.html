Project Documentation & Findings

Project Overview

This project investigates how people organise information spatially and the cognitive strategies they employ when solving card arrangement tasks.

Participants used an interactive 8×8 grid to arrange playing cards (Kings, Queens, Jacks, and blank cards), while every movement was tracked.

Core question: what separates successful problem-solvers from those who struggle?

What We Measured

229 trials across four conditions:

ConditionDescription
KQKing & Queen only
KQBKing, Queen & Blank
KQJKing, Queen & Jack
KQJBKing, Queen, Jack & Blank

Nine Analysis Views

  1. Successful Clean (15+) — organised play
  2. Failed Messy (<15) — unstable organisation
  3. All Successful — winning paths
  4. Early vs Late — progression phases
  5. Opening Strategies — first 5 moves
  6. Retry & Recovery — repeated attempts
  7. Extreme Cases — cleanest vs messiest
  8. Speed Comparison — quick vs slow
  9. Repetition Patterns — focused vs broad

Key Findings

Organisation > Effort

Failures showed 63% faster organisational decline (p = 0.0085). Success was about staying systematic.

Blank Card Advantage

Blank card users: +29–36% success rate uplift (χ², p < 0.01).

Learning is Rare

Only 14% of failures recovered on later attempts.

Messiness Predicts Outcome

Clean trials (< 2.0): 68% success. Messy (> 3.5): 18%.

Practical Implications

  • Training should emphasise organisational strategy, not repetition
  • Flexible tools (blank cards) materially improve outcomes
  • Early intervention matters — strategy recovery is rare
  • Messiness acts as an early warning signal of struggle
